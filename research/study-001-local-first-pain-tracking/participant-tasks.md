# Participant Tasks

## Purpose

These tasks are designed to evaluate whether participants can understand, use, and trust PainTracker during first use.

Participants may use fictional, sample, or approximate information. They do not need to disclose real medical details.

The moderator should observe task completion, confusion points, time on task, errors, hesitation, and participant comments.

---

# Task 1: Explore the Dashboard

## Participant Instruction

Open the app and look at the main screen.

Please describe:

1. What you think this app is for.
2. What sections or buttons you notice first.
3. Which parts look most useful for tracking pain.
4. Which parts are unclear or confusing.

## Success Criteria

The participant can identify the app’s basic purpose and at least one main pain tracking area.

## Data to Record

- Time to first meaningful understanding
- Sections noticed first
- Confusing labels or layout elements
- Whether participant understands what to do next
- Any comments about visual clarity, trust, or overwhelm

## Protective Computing Focus

- Cognitive protection
- First-use clarity
- Routine logging confidence

---

# Task 2: Log a New Pain Entry

## Participant Instruction

Create a new pain entry.

You may use fictional or approximate information. You do not need to share private medical details.

Please complete the entry using values that make sense to you.

## Success Criteria

The participant creates and saves a complete pain entry without critical assistance.

## Data to Record

- Task completion status
- Time to complete entry
- Number of errors
- Number of moderator assists
- Fields that caused hesitation
- Whether the participant understood when the entry was saved
- Whether the process felt too long, too personal, or too mentally heavy

## Protective Computing Focus

- Core usability
- Cognitive load
- Consent clarity
- Routine logging burden

---

# Task 3: Review a Past Entry

## Participant Instruction

Find a previous pain entry and open it.

Please describe:

1. What information is easy to find.
2. What information is difficult to find.
3. Whether the entry summary makes sense.
4. Whether you would trust this record later.

## Success Criteria

The participant can locate and review a previous entry.

## Data to Record

- Whether participant finds the entry
- Time to locate entry
- Confusion around history, labels, or navigation
- Whether entry details are understandable
- Whether participant trusts the saved record
- Comments about editing, correcting, or reviewing data

## Protective Computing Focus

- Reversible state
- Record clarity
- User control
- Long-term documentation trust

---

# Task 4: Find Privacy and Data Storage Information

## Participant Instruction

Find where the app explains privacy, data storage, or data control.

After reviewing it, please explain in your own words:

1. Where you think your data is stored.
2. Whether you think the app uploads your data.
3. Whether you feel in control of what is saved or shared.
4. What still feels unclear.

## Success Criteria

The participant can find privacy information and correctly explain the local-first storage model.

## Data to Record

- Whether participant finds privacy information
- Time to find privacy information
- Whether participant correctly understands local storage
- Whether participant believes data is uploaded
- Trust rating before and after explanation, if applicable
- Remaining privacy concerns
- Comments about cloud accounts, sharing, exports, or institutional access

## Protective Computing Focus

- Local authority
- Exposure minimization
- Consent clarity
- Trust formation

---

# Task 5: Preview or Generate a Report

## Participant Instruction

Find the feature for creating, previewing, or exporting a report.

Please describe:

1. What you think the report is for.
2. What information appears to be included.
3. Whether you would feel comfortable sharing it.
4. Who you might share it with, such as a doctor, support person, advocate, caseworker, or other trusted person.

You do not need to actually share or send anything.

## Success Criteria

The participant can locate the report feature and understand its purpose.

## Data to Record

- Whether participant finds report feature
- Time to preview or generate report
- Confusion around export wording
- Whether participant understands what is included
- Whether participant feels in control of sharing
- Export confidence rating
- Concerns about oversharing, formatting, accuracy, or audience

## Protective Computing Focus

- Selective disclosure
- Export confidence
- Consent clarity
- External documentation trust

---

# Task 6: Recover From a Mistake

## Participant Instruction

Imagine you entered something incorrectly.

Please try to find a way to correct, edit, cancel, delete, or recover from the mistake.

## Success Criteria

The participant can identify a recovery path or explain what they would expect to happen.

## Data to Record

- Whether participant finds edit or correction options
- Whether recovery feels obvious
- Whether participant fears data loss
- Whether labels around delete, cancel, save, or edit are clear
- Any comments about control, anxiety, or confidence

## Protective Computing Focus

- Reversible state
- Failure recovery
- User control
- Anxiety reduction

---

# Task 7: Provide Open Feedback

## Participant Instruction

Please describe your overall experience using the app.

You can comment on anything, including what felt helpful, confusing, safe, unsafe, trustworthy, frustrating, or missing.

Please answer:

1. What was the easiest part?
2. What was the most confusing part?
3. Did the local-first privacy model affect your trust?
4. What would make you more likely to use this regularly?
5. What would make you stop using it?
6. What one change would improve the app the most?

## Success Criteria

Participant provides open feedback about usability, trust, privacy, and routine use.

## Data to Record

- Strongest positive finding
- Strongest friction point
- Privacy or trust comments
- Routine logging barriers
- Safety or discomfort comments
- Suggested improvements
- Participant’s one highest-priority change

## Protective Computing Focus

- Participant authority
- Trust under stress
- Routine adoption
- Safety perception
