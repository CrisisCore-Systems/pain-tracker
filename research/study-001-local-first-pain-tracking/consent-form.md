# Participant Consent Form

## Study Title

Local First Pain Tracking Usability and Trust Study

## Study Overview

You are invited to take part in a study about a pain tracking app called PainTracker.

PainTracker is designed to help people document pain while keeping their information under their own control. The purpose of this study is to understand whether the app is easy to use, whether people understand how their data is stored, and whether the local first privacy model increases trust and perceived safety.

This study is focused on usability, privacy understanding, and participant experience. It is not a medical treatment study.

## Why You Are Being Asked to Participate

You are being invited because you may have experience with pain tracking, health documentation, disability related paperwork, privacy concerns, or self documentation tools.

You do not need to disclose private medical details to participate.

## What Participation Involves

If you agree to participate, you will be asked to:

1. Use the PainTracker app during a guided session.
2. Complete a small set of tasks, such as creating a pain entry, finding privacy information, and previewing an exportable report.
3. Answer short survey questions about trust, privacy, cognitive load, and ease of use.
4. Share feedback about what felt clear, confusing, safe, unsafe, useful, or frustrating.

The session will take approximately 45 to 60 minutes.

You may skip any question or task that makes you uncomfortable.

## Voluntary Participation

Your participation is completely voluntary.

You may stop participating at any time, for any reason, without penalty. You do not have to explain why you are stopping.

Choosing not to participate, skipping a question, or withdrawing from the study will not affect your access to PainTracker or any future support related to the app.

## What Data Will Be Collected

The study may collect:

1. Task completion results.
2. Approximate time needed to complete tasks.
3. Notes about where confusion, hesitation, or errors occurred.
4. Survey responses.
5. Interview notes.
6. Optional audio recording, only if you separately agree.
7. Optional short quotes from your feedback, only in deidentified form.

The study will not intentionally collect your full medical history, diagnosis records, legal records, income records, government records, or private health files.

You should not enter real medical details unless you choose to do so. You may use sample or fictional information during the session.

## Privacy and Confidentiality

Your identity will be protected in all reports.

Your name and contact information will be stored separately from study notes whenever possible. Reports will use participant codes instead of names.

The final study report may include summarized findings, such as task completion rates, common usability issues, trust ratings, and recurring feedback themes.

The report will not identify you personally.

## Data Storage

Study notes and survey responses will be stored securely by the research lead.

Identifying information will be removed from analysis files where possible.

Identifiable study records will be retained for up to 12 months unless you separately agree to future contact or follow up.

After that period, identifiable study records will be deleted unless you have separately agreed to future contact or follow up.

## Withdrawal and Data Removal

You may withdraw during the session or after the session by contacting the research lead.

If you withdraw before analysis begins, your identifiable data can be removed.

If you withdraw after the data has already been anonymized or included in aggregate analysis, it may not be possible to remove your data because it may no longer be linked to your identity.

## Risks

The risks of this study are expected to be low.

Possible risks include:

1. Mild discomfort when thinking about pain, health documentation, disability, privacy, or past experiences with institutions.
2. Frustration if the app is confusing or does not work as expected.
3. Concern about privacy or data control.

You may pause, skip a question, or stop the session at any time.

This study does not require you to discuss traumatic events, private diagnoses, legal matters, or detailed medical history.

## Benefits

There may be no direct personal benefit to you.

Your feedback may help improve PainTracker and support the development of safer local first tools for people who document pain, disability, health symptoms, or sensitive personal information.

## Compensation

No compensation will be provided.

## Limits of Confidentiality

The research lead will protect your privacy as much as possible.

However, confidentiality may have limits if disclosure is required by law, such as an immediate risk of serious harm to yourself or another person.
