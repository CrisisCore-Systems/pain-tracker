# Measures

## Purpose

This document defines the quantitative and qualitative measures used in the Local First Pain Tracking Usability and Trust Study.

The measures are designed to evaluate:

1. Usability
2. Task completion
3. Trust in local data control
4. Privacy anxiety
5. Cognitive burden
6. Export confidence
7. Routine logging potential
8. Protective Computing alignment

The study does not measure clinical effectiveness, diagnosis, treatment outcome, or medical improvement.

---

# 1. Quantitative Measures

## 1.1 Task Completion

| Measure | Description | Collection Method | Success Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| task_completion_rate | Percentage of participants who complete each task | Moderator observation | 80 percent or higher |
| core_logging_success | Whether participant completes a pain entry | Moderator observation | 80 percent or higher |
| report_generation_success | Whether participant can preview or create an exportable report | Moderator observation | 80 percent or higher |
| privacy_info_success | Whether participant can find the privacy or data storage explanation | Moderator observation | 80 percent or higher |

## 1.2 Time on Task

| Measure | Description | Collection Method | Success Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| task_duration_seconds | Time from task start to task completion | Timestamp or stopwatch | Recorded for all tasks |
| core_logging_duration | Time to complete pain entry | Timestamp or stopwatch | Under 5 minutes |
| privacy_info_duration | Time to find privacy information | Timestamp or stopwatch | Under 2 minutes |
| report_generation_duration | Time to generate or preview report | Timestamp or stopwatch | Under 5 minutes |

## 1.3 Error and Assistance Measures

| Measure | Description | Collection Method | Success Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| error_count | Number of observable errors during a task | Moderator observation | Low |
| retry_count | Number of repeated attempts | Moderator observation | Low |
| moderator_assists | Number of times participant requires help | Moderator observation | Low |
| failed_task_count | Number of tasks participant cannot complete | Moderator observation | Zero critical task failures preferred |

Errors may include:

1. Clicking the wrong area repeatedly
2. Misunderstanding a label
3. Entering information in the wrong place
4. Becoming lost in navigation
5. Failing to understand what happened after an action
6. Being unable to recover from a mistake

# 3. Qualitative Measures

Qualitative data will be collected through observation, participant comments, and post session questions.

## 3.1 Think Aloud Comments

Participants may be asked to describe what they are thinking while completing tasks.

Record comments related to:

1. Confusion
2. Trust
3. Privacy
4. Navigation
5. Pain logging flow
6. Report generation
7. Data storage understanding
8. Emotional comfort or discomfort
9. Missing features
10. Reasons they would or would not use the app

## 3.2 Observed Confusion Points

The moderator records moments where the participant:

1. Pauses for a long time
2. Clicks repeatedly without progress
3. Misunderstands a label
4. Asks what something means
5. Cannot find a feature
6. Expresses uncertainty
7. Shows frustration
8. Stops or skips a task

## 3.3 Privacy and Trust Feedback

Capture participant comments about:

1. Local storage
2. No account requirement
3. Data control
4. Exporting information
5. Fear of cloud storage
6. Fear of institutional access
7. Whether the privacy explanation was believable
8. Whether the app felt safer than other tracking tools

## 3.4 Routine Use Feedback

Capture participant comments about:

1. Whether they would use the app daily or weekly
2. What would make them stop using it
3. What would make the habit easier
4. Whether the app feels too heavy for regular use
5. Whether reminders, templates, or faster entry would help

## 3.5 Safety and Discomfort Notes

Record only deidentified summaries.

Capture whether the participant:

1. Became uncomfortable
2. Wanted to skip a question
3. Felt exposed
4. Felt pressured
5. Felt the app asked for too much
6. Expressed concern about sensitive data
7. Needed a pause
8. Chose to stop the session

Do not collect unnecessary personal details.

## 4.2 Open Ended Questions

Participants answer in their own words.

1. What was the easiest part of using the app?
2. What was the most confusing part?
3. Did the local first privacy model affect your trust? If yes, how?
4. What privacy concerns did you still have?
5. What would make you more likely to use this regularly?
6. What would make you stop using it?
7. Would you trust the report for a medical, disability, workplace, legal, or advocacy context? Why or why not?
8. What should be changed before this app is tested with more people?

# 5. Protective Computing Measures

The study also evaluates whether PainTracker aligns with Protective Computing principles.

| Principle | Measure |
|---|---|
| Local authority | Participant understands data is stored locally and feels in control |
| Exposure minimization | Participant does not feel pressured to disclose unnecessary information |
| Consent clarity | Participant understands what is stored, exported, and shared |
| Reversible state | Participant can edit, review, or recover without fear |
| Explicit degradation | Participant understands what happens if something fails |
| Cognitive protection | Participant does not feel overwhelmed by the interface |
| Trust under stress | Participant believes the app could still be used while tired, stressed, or in pain |

# 7. Success Thresholds

The pilot will be considered successful if:

1. At least 80 percent of participants complete the core pain logging task.
2. Average core logging time is under 5 minutes.
3. At least 80 percent of participants can correctly explain where their data is stored.
4. Average trust score is 4 out of 5 or higher.
5. Average perceived safety score is 4 out of 5 or higher.
6. Average privacy anxiety score is 2 out of 5 or lower.
7. No critical safety, privacy, or data loss concern is observed.

The pilot will require redesign before expansion if:

1. Multiple participants cannot find or understand the privacy explanation.
2. Participants believe their data is being uploaded when it is not.
3. Export generation causes major confusion.
4. Participants feel pressured to disclose more than they want.
5. Navigation blocks core logging.
6. Any participant reports feeling unsafe, exposed, or misled by the app.
