# Recruitment Script

## Study Title

Study 001: Local First Pain Tracking

## Short Recruitment Message

Hello, my name is K. Overton.

I am running a small independent usability study for PainTracker, a local first pain tracking app designed to help people document pain while keeping their information under their own control.

I am looking for adults who live with chronic pain, recurring pain, disability related symptoms, or ongoing symptom tracking needs to try the app and share feedback.

This is not a medical treatment study. It does not test whether PainTracker reduces pain or improves health outcomes. The study only looks at usability, privacy understanding, trust, perceived safety, and barriers to routine use.

You do not need to share your diagnosis, medical history, legal history, income status, government records, or private health records. You may use fictional, sample, or approximate information during the session.

## What Participation Involves

Participation involves one remote session lasting approximately 45 to 60 minutes.

During the session, you will be asked to:

1. Try a few basic tasks in the app.
2. Create or review a sample pain entry.
3. Find privacy or data storage information.
4. Preview or locate a report feature.
5. Answer short questions about usability, trust, privacy, and comfort.
6. Share feedback about what felt clear, confusing, useful, unsafe, or difficult.

Participation is voluntary.

You may skip any question, pause, or stop at any time without penalty.

## Compensation

No compensation will be provided.

This is an unfunded independent usability study conducted by a solo developer. Because there is no compensation, you should only take part if you are comfortable volunteering feedback to help improve local first pain tracking tools.

## Eligibility

To participate, you must be:

1. Age 18 or older.
2. Living with chronic pain, recurring pain, disability related symptoms, or ongoing symptom tracking needs.
3. Comfortable using a web based app during a remote session.
4. Able to provide informed consent.

You do not need technical experience.

## Privacy

The study is designed to collect only the minimum information needed to understand usability and trust.

You may use fictional or sample pain information during testing.

Your name will not be used in public reports. Findings will be summarized in deidentified form.

Optional audio recording, use of deidentified quotes, and future contact will each require separate consent.
