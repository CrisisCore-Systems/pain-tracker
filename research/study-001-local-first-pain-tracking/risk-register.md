# Risk Register

## Study 001: Local First Pain Tracking

This risk register identifies participant, privacy, technical, recruitment, and research integrity risks for Study 001.

Risk levels should be reviewed before recruitment, during sessions, and before publication of results.

# R001: Participant Discomfort

## Description

Participants may feel uncomfortable discussing pain, disability, health documentation, privacy, or prior experiences with institutions.

## Likelihood

Medium

## Impact

Medium

## Mitigation

Participants will be reminded that:

1. They do not need to share private medical details.
2. They may use fictional, sample, or approximate data.
3. They may skip any question.
4. They may pause or stop the session at any time.
5. The study is about app usability and trust, not medical evaluation.

## Response Plan

If discomfort occurs, the moderator should pause the session, offer to skip the task, and remind the participant they may stop without penalty.

---

# R002: Privacy Concern or Perceived Exposure

## Description

Participants may worry that sensitive information is being collected, uploaded, analyzed, or shared.

## Likelihood

Medium

## Impact

High

## Mitigation

The consent form and session introduction must clearly explain:

1. PainTracker app data remains local unless the participant chooses to export or share it.
2. Research data is separate from app data.
3. The study does not require real medical information.
4. Public reports will use deidentified summaries.
5. Direct quotes require separate permission.

## Response Plan

If a participant expresses concern, the moderator should clarify what is collected, what is not collected, and what remains on the participant’s device.

---

# R003: Accidental Collection of Sensitive Health Details

## Description

Participants may disclose more personal health information than needed during testing.

## Likelihood

Medium

## Impact

High

## Mitigation

The moderator should state before tasks begin:

> You may use fictional or sample information. You do not need to disclose your diagnosis, medical history, legal history, income status, or private records.

Research notes should summarize usability relevant information only.

## Response Plan

If unnecessary sensitive details are disclosed, do not repeat them in the analysis file. Replace them with a minimal deidentified summary.

---

# R004: Confusion About Local First Storage

## Description

Participants may misunderstand where their data is stored or believe the app uploads data when it does not.

## Likelihood

Medium

## Impact

High

## Mitigation

The study includes a specific task asking participants to find and explain the privacy and data storage model.

The app should include plain language privacy information explaining:

1. What stays on device.
2. What is not uploaded.
3. What exports do.
4. What the user controls.
5. What happens if the app fails.

## Response Plan

Record the confusion as a study finding. If multiple participants misunderstand local storage, the privacy explanation must be redesigned before expansion.

---

# R005: Technical Issues

## Description

Browser, device, internet, screen sharing, or app errors may interrupt the session.

## Likelihood

Medium

## Impact

Medium

## Mitigation

Prepare:

1. Backup browser option.
2. Backup device option if available.
3. Restart instructions.
4. Manual observation notes.
5. Demo data fallback.
6. Session rescheduling option.

## Response Plan

If a technical issue blocks task completion, mark the task as interrupted by technical error rather than participant failure.

---

# R006: Misunderstood Instructions

## Description

Participants may misinterpret task directions or become unsure what they are being asked to do.

## Likelihood

Medium

## Impact

Low to Medium

## Mitigation

Use short, plain language task instructions.

Participants may ask clarifying questions.

The moderator should avoid coaching unless needed, and any assistance should be recorded.

## Response Plan

If the participant misunderstands the task, record whether the issue came from the task script, app wording, or participant uncertainty.

---

# R007: Recruitment Shortfall

## Description

The study may not recruit enough eligible participants.

## Likelihood

Medium

## Impact

Medium

## Mitigation

Use multiple outreach channels:

1. Chronic pain communities.
2. Disability communities.
3. Privacy focused groups.
4. Personal networks.
5. Open source or local first software communities.
6. Social posts.

Extend the recruitment window if needed.

## Response Plan

If fewer than 8 participants are recruited, report the study as a smaller formative usability test rather than a full pilot.

---

# R008: Recruitment Bias From No Compensation

## Description

Because no compensation is provided, the sample may overrepresent people who have enough time, energy, stability, or interest to volunteer without payment.

## Likelihood

High

## Impact

Medium

## Mitigation

Recruitment and consent materials must clearly state:

> No compensation will be provided. This is an unfunded independent study conducted by a solo developer.

The results report must list this as a limitation.

## Response Plan

Do not generalize findings too broadly. Treat results as early exploratory evidence only.

---

# R009: Accessibility Barriers

## Description

Participants may experience access barriers related to pain, fatigue, vision, mobility, cognition, device type, language, or assistive technology.

## Likelihood

Medium

## Impact

High

## Mitigation

Before the session, ask:

1. Do you need any accessibility support for the session?
2. Would you prefer a slower pace?
3. Do you need breaks?
4. Are you using a phone, tablet, laptop, or desktop?

Record accessibility barriers as findings.

## Response Plan

If accessibility prevents task completion, mark the issue as an app or session design barrier, not participant failure.

---

# R010: Audio Recording Discomfort

## Description

Participants may feel uncomfortable being recorded.

## Likelihood

Medium

## Impact

Medium

## Mitigation

Audio recording must be optional and separately consented to.

Participants may still participate without recording.

Written notes can be used instead.

## Response Plan

If recording consent is not granted, do not record. Use written notes only.

---

# R011: Misuse or Overstatement of Findings

## Description

The study findings may be overstated as proof of clinical benefit, safety, or superiority over cloud tools.

## Likelihood

Medium

## Impact

High

## Mitigation

The report must clearly state that Study 001 does not prove:

1. Clinical effectiveness.
2. Pain reduction.
3. Improved health outcomes.
4. Long term adherence.
5. Superiority over all cloud based health apps.

## Response Plan

Include a “What Remains Unproven” section in all public reporting.

---

# R012: Solo Developer Trust Concern

## Description

Participants or future reviewers may question the independence, rigor, or safety of a study conducted by a solo developer.

## Likelihood

Medium

## Impact

Medium

## Mitigation

Be transparent that the study is:

1. Independent.
2. Unfunded.
3. Pre pilot.
4. Not institutionally reviewed unless that changes.
5. Designed to collect minimum necessary data.

Maintain clear documentation in the public research folder.

## Response Plan

Use Study 001 as a formative pilot and seek external review or partnership before larger research claims.

---

# Escalation Criteria

The study should pause or require redesign if:

1. Any participant reports feeling unsafe, exposed, pressured, or misled.
2. Multiple participants misunderstand where their data is stored.
3. Participants believe data is uploaded when it is not.
4. Export features create privacy fear or oversharing concern.
5. Accessibility barriers prevent meaningful participation.
6. Technical failures make the session unreliable.
7. The study process starts collecting more sensitive data than necessary.

---

# Risk Review Schedule

| Stage | Review Action |
|---|---|
| Before recruitment | Confirm consent, recruitment, and task scripts disclose key risks |
| Before each session | Confirm participant consent and access needs |
| During each session | Monitor discomfort, confusion, and privacy concern |
| After each session | Log risks, technical issues, and mitigation needs |
| Before publication | Review findings for overstatement, identifiability, and scope limits |

---

# Core Rule

A study about protective software must not become extractive.

If the research process pressures participants, collects unnecessary sensitive data, hides uncertainty, or overclaims the findings, it violates the thing it is trying to prove.
