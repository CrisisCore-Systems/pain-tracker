# 30 Day Patient Report Clarity Pilot

## Pilot Name

30 Day Patient Report Clarity Pilot

## Objective

Test whether PainTracker.ca helps a small group of interested patients or injured workers arrive with clearer self-reported symptom summaries before, during, or after pain and medication management style care.

The pilot is designed to evaluate clarity and burden, not clinical outcomes.

## Participants

5 to 10 interested workers or patients who:

- Are comfortable using a browser-based tool.
- Want a private way to record pain and function between appointments.
- Understand that PainTracker.ca is optional and not a clinical requirement.
- Understand that they choose what to export or show.

## No Integration Required

The pilot does not require integration with clinic systems, electronic medical records, insurer systems, employer systems, or WorkSafeBC systems.

Patients use PainTracker.ca independently. Any report shown to staff is patient generated and patient controlled.

## What Patients Do

Participants are invited to:

- Use PainTracker.ca privately for 30 days.
- Record pain severity, location, symptoms, triggers, medication notes, sleep impact, and functional impact when useful.
- Generate an optional summary before an appointment or review conversation.
- Decide what, if anything, they want to share with staff.

## What Staff Do

Staff may:

- Provide the PainTracker.ca link and a plain-language privacy note.
- Clarify that use is optional.
- Review patient-generated summaries only when a patient chooses to provide one.
- Complete a short optional feedback form about clarity, usefulness, and burden.

Staff are not asked to monitor the app, manage accounts, troubleshoot devices, or receive automatic data.

## What CrisisCore Systems Provides

CrisisCore Systems can provide:

- A one-page patient handout.
- A privacy and data handling sheet.
- A sample worker report.
- Screenshot demo material.
- Suggested wording for staff who want to introduce the tool neutrally.
- A short feedback form.
- A follow-up meeting after the pilot window.

## Optional Clinician Feedback Form

Suggested questions:

1. Did the patient-generated report make symptom history easier to understand?
2. Was the report concise enough to review in a normal appointment workflow?
3. Did the report create extra administrative burden?
4. Were any sections unclear or unnecessary?
5. Would a refined version be useful for similar patients?

## Success Signals

The pilot is successful if staff report that:

- Patient summaries were easier to review than fragmented notes or memory-only recall.
- Reports were concise enough for real appointment constraints.
- Patients understood that sharing was optional.
- Staff did not need system integration or account management.
- The tool reduced confusion without creating new administrative burden.

The pilot does not claim to prove improved clinical outcomes, treatment adherence, claim outcomes, or program efficiency.

## Privacy Boundaries

- PainTracker.ca core use does not require an account.
- Patient data is stored locally on the user's device.
- Nothing is automatically shared with The Newly Institute, CrisisCore Systems, employers, insurers, or WorkSafeBC.
- Patients choose what to export or show.
- Staff should treat any report as supplementary patient-reported information.

## Exit Criteria

The pilot should stop or be reconsidered if:

- Patients misunderstand the tool as mandatory.
- Staff feel pressured to treat reports as clinical records.
- The workflow adds meaningful administrative burden.
- Privacy boundaries are unclear to patients or staff.
- Patients cannot use the tool safely or comfortably in their circumstances.

## Follow-Up Meeting

After 30 days, CrisisCore Systems can meet with the appropriate contact for 20 to 30 minutes to review:

- Whether the reports were clear.
- Whether the burden was acceptable.
- What should be removed, simplified, or clarified.
- Whether a refined handout or future small pilot would be useful.
