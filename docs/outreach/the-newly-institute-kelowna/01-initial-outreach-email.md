## Email Copy

Hello,

My name is K Overton. I am the founder of CrisisCore Systems and the creator of PainTracker.ca, a free, private, browser-based pain tracking app for people living with chronic pain, injury recovery, flare patterns, and work-related pain documentation.

I am reaching out because PainTracker.ca may be relevant to patients participating in pain and medication management style care, especially injured workers who arrive at appointments with fragmented memory, inconsistent notes, or no usable record of symptom patterns.

PainTracker.ca is not a diagnostic tool and does not replace clinical judgement. Its purpose is simpler: it gives patients a private way to record the daily details that often get lost before they reach a clinician, including pain severity, location, symptoms, triggers, medication notes, sleep impact, functional impact, and daily context. Patients can then generate cleaner summaries or reports to bring into appointments if they choose.

The core app works offline, stores data locally on the user's device, and does not require an account for core use. Nothing is automatically shared with a clinic, insurer, employer, or WorkSafeBC. The patient chooses what to export or show.

I would be grateful if the right person at The Newly Institute Kelowna would be willing to briefly review the package below and let me know whether a small, low-burden patient report clarity pilot would be worth discussing. The pilot I am suggesting would not require integration with clinic systems. It would simply invite 5 to 10 interested patients or workers to use PainTracker.ca privately for 30 days, then bring an optional patient-generated summary to a visit or review conversation.
