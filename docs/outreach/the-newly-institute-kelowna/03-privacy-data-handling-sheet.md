# PainTracker.ca Privacy And Data Handling Sheet

## Plain Language Summary

PainTracker.ca is designed so the patient keeps control of their pain records. Core tracking happens in the browser, data is stored locally on the user's device, and no account is required for core use.

The app does not automatically share pain entries, notes, reports, or health information with clinics, insurers, employers, WorkSafeBC, or CrisisCore Systems. A patient chooses what to export, save, print, email, or show.

## For Patients

PainTracker.ca lets you record pain and symptom details privately on your own device. You can use it to keep track of pain severity, location, symptoms, triggers, medication notes, sleep impact, and functional impact.

If you want to bring a summary to an appointment, you can choose to export or show a report. You do not have to share anything unless you decide to.

## For Providers And Program Staff

PainTracker.ca is best understood as a patient-controlled tracking and summary tool. It may help patients arrive with clearer self-reported information, especially when memory, pain, sleep disruption, or medication changes make appointment recall difficult.

It is not a clinic portal, diagnostic tool, electronic medical record, treatment recommendation engine, or replacement for clinical judgement.

## Local-First Storage

PainTracker.ca stores core tracking data locally on the user's device through browser storage. The patient remains responsible for the device, browser profile, backups, and any files they choose to export.

This means:

- A clinic does not automatically receive patient data.
- CrisisCore Systems does not automatically receive patient pain entries.
- A patient can use the core tracking app without creating an account.
- If a patient changes devices, clears browser storage, loses the device, or loses access to the browser profile, local records may be affected unless the patient has exported or backed up their data.

## Offline Capability

PainTracker.ca is designed to keep core tracking useful when internet access is weak or unavailable. Patients can continue using the app locally where supported by their device and browser.

Offline capability does not mean every optional feature works without internet. Optional features that depend on network access should be treated as secondary to core local tracking.

## User-Controlled Sharing

PainTracker.ca does not automatically share data with:

- The Newly Institute.
- Any clinic or care provider.
- Any employer.
- Any insurer.
- WorkSafeBC.
- CrisisCore Systems.

The user chooses what to export, print, email, save, or show. Exported files leave the local app boundary only when the user takes action.

## No Unnecessary Account Requirement For Core Use

Core tracking does not require a patient to create an account, provide an email address, or register before making a useful record.

This matters for patients who need a low-friction way to capture symptoms before they are ready to set up another service account.

## What Patients May Track

Patients may choose to record:

- Pain severity.
- Pain locations.
- Symptoms.
- Triggers and daily context.
- Medication notes.
- Sleep impact.
- Functional impact.
- Flare notes.
- Appointment questions.

Patients should avoid entering information they do not want stored on the device or included in an export.

## Limits And Responsibilities

PainTracker.ca is not a medical record system and is not intended to replace clinical documentation.

Patients remain responsible for:

- Deciding what to enter.
- Keeping their device secure.
- Managing exported files.
- Choosing what to share.
- Backing up records if they want long-term portability.

Providers remain responsible for:

- Reviewing patient-provided information using clinical judgement.
- Confirming clinically relevant information through appropriate assessment.
- Treating patient-generated reports as supplementary self-reported information.

## Practical Privacy Boundary

The safest default is simple:

The patient records privately.
The patient reviews privately.
The patient decides what to share.
The clinic receives only what the patient chooses to provide.
