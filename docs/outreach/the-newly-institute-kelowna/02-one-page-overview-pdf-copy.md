### What It Is

PainTracker.ca is a free, private, browser-based pain tracking app for people living with chronic pain, injury recovery, flare patterns, and work-related pain documentation.

It helps users record daily pain details, review patterns, and create cleaner summaries they can bring to appointments. It is not a diagnostic tool and does not replace clinical judgement.

### Who It Helps

PainTracker.ca may help patients or injured workers who:

- Struggle to remember symptoms clearly during appointments.
- Have fluctuating pain, flare patterns, or medication changes.
- Need a more consistent record of pain severity, location, triggers, function, and sleep.
- Arrive with scattered notes, incomplete memory, or no usable symptom history.
- Want a private tool that does not require an account for core tracking.

### Why It Matters In Pain Care

Many injured workers and chronic pain patients arrive at care conversations with fragmented recall. Pain, poor sleep, medication changes, stress, and long wait times can make it difficult to explain what happened between appointments.

PainTracker.ca gives patients a low-friction way to capture the daily details that often get lost before they reach a clinician or coordinator.

### What Workers Can Track

- Pain severity and trend over time.
- Pain location and body area.
- Symptoms and flare notes.
- Triggers, activities, and daily context.
- Medication notes and perceived timing effects.
- Functional impact, including work and daily activity limitations.
- Sleep impact and fatigue context.
- Appointment questions or discussion prompts.

### What Clinicians May Receive

If the patient chooses to export or show a report, clinicians or program staff may receive:

- A date-bounded patient-generated summary.
- Pain severity trend and common pain locations.
- Functional impact notes.
- Medication and treatment notes entered by the patient.
- Flare and trigger observations.
- Appointment discussion prompts.

This is patient-reported tracking, not a medical record and not a clinical decision system.

### Privacy And Access Boundaries

- Core tracking works without an account.
- Data is stored locally on the user's device.
- The app is offline capable.
- Nothing is automatically shared with clinics, insurers, employers, or WorkSafeBC.
- The user chooses what to export, save, print, email, or show.
- Optional exports should be treated as patient-supplied documents.

### Suggested Low-Burden Pilot

30 Day Patient Report Clarity Pilot

- 5 to 10 interested patients or injured workers.
- No clinic system integration required.
- Patients use PainTracker.ca privately for 30 days.
- Patients may bring an optional report to an appointment or review conversation.
- Staff may complete a short optional feedback form about clarity, usefulness, and burden.
- CrisisCore Systems provides setup notes, privacy wording, sample reports, and a follow-up review call.
